About the model:

The basis of the Naive Bayes classifier is in Bayes' Theorem, an example of probabilistic theory. This theorem calculates the likelihood of an event based on past knowledge of possible event-related situations.

To utilize this model in the best way possible, the input article should be between 4000 and 6000 characters. It will work on the smaller or bigger number of character but not as accurately, and if possible provide articles title speratated with "-".

This means that, in terms of text classification, the likelihood that a given text belongs in a specific category (in our case, "fake" or "real" news) is ascertained by looking at its frequency and word distribution.

The word "naive" is employed since it is thought that the features that comprise the model are independent of one other. Put differently, modifying the value of a single feature does not directly affect the values of any other features that the algorithm utilizes.
